Dinamundo

Checking into People

About the Project

Dinamundo is an iPhone app that uses mobile technology to connect people through fun, light-weight moments. The brainchild of Founder and CEO, Erik Blachford, Dinamundo's core mission was to use smartphones to encourage real-world connections, beginning with the traveler to local dynamic. The challenge was to build a light-weight game that would incentize these groups to meet in person for the ostensible purposes of advancement in the game. Once together, the players use the app to check in together - checking in to people, not places - and the app delivers fun and engagement through interpersonal connection.

My Role

I was the interaction, visual designer, and head of Product under the direction of Founder and CEO, Erik Blachford for the projct duration of 6 months (Jan 2012 - June 2012). I helped define the product core principles, lay out the strategic roadmap, and translate it all into actionable designs and development specifications. I also created all visual assets and managed execution to a timely release.

Introduction

An Unconventional Beginning

Dinamundo started back in 2011 with the mission of using modern technology to enhance and facilitate meaningful interactions in the real world, rather than distracting us from them. Founder Erik Blachford (formed Expedia CEO), knew his way around the travel space and saw an opportunity to crack the traveler/local meet up nut with using the mechanics of a check in game to incentivize people to meet in person. I first heard of the app in October 2011 from a friend and colleague, Ed Park, had been peripherally involved. When Ed first told me the product, I was immediately enthralled with

the idea of checking into people. But the first version had been released as a Facebook app and, while the concepts were there, the experience was a bit clunky. As Ed walked me through the app, we began to imagine what it might look like as a mobile experience. Bursting with ideas, I went home that night and couldn't help myself from drawing up a few wireframes. I sent them over to Ed and, to my surprise, he had coded the wireframes into a functional prototype the next day!

Research

Digging In Deep

The prototype had convinced the product owner, Erik Blachford, to officially fund the development of the Dinamundo project rather than wrap the project up as he had previously been thinking. It had helped him see just how much potential there as to the concept and he had realized that, with the right team and resources behind it, it deserved a real shot. Rather than jump right in to building the prototype though, he wanted to make sure our decisions were founded in solid research. And so my first task was to conduct an indepth market analysis of both the

travel and checkin space in order to determine the right product positioning as well as gain a full understanding of the target user. The first purpose of my research was to analyze check in functionalities in the context of the current travel landscape. I wanted to answer questions like - where and how are users checking in? How do current travel patterns influence where and how users will check in abroad? The second goal was to understand who the Dinamundo users might be and what their check in experience should look like.

Market Analysis

As a first step, I conducted an indepth full market analysis, sourcing as much quantitative information as I could find related to the both the travel industry and the check in space. I summarized the key findings into an infographic-like reference packet for the team.

Ethnographic Research

Simultaneously, I became an avid user of all apps related to tracking travel information and checking into places.

Strategy

Defining Our User

Pulling togethering information from articles, blogs, posts, etc as well as the personal useage and user interviews, I found that there were four main motivations for using a social check in app. These were the principles on which we would base the strategy for our app. Scanning a QR code to unlock an amusing fortune cookie message isn't splitting the atom, but it's the kind of thing that millions of people do every day by habit when they check their horoscopes, glance and their favorite commedy sites, or check in on the reliably amusing posters in their Twitter feed. By basing our design and functionality on these four principles, we believed we had a winning strategy for the app.

Social Broadcasting

Checking in affords bragging rights and opportunities for self branding. People like to show that they've been meeting up with friends, share the experience with others.

Competitive Momentum

Some individuals are motivated to check in and compete against friends for points, earn unique badges, each status such as mayorship.

Opportunistic Intrigue

Another important user motivation is to find deals, get info about location, see tips from friends or other users.

Personal Fulfillment

Last, people often check in to keep track of happenings, maintain a digital scrapbook, or take notes on certain events.
