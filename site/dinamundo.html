Dinamundo

Checking into People

About the Project

Dinamundo is an iPhone app that uses mobile technology to connect people through fun, light-weight moments. I was the interaction and visual designer for the product that released May 2012.

My Role

As Co-Founder and Head of Product, I managed the product roadmap, led the design process, oversaw the development and kept everyone running in the same direction in a fast paced environment. I also created the website flows, interactions and layouts from the ground up, and became responsible for the visual design and user operations after launch.

Introduction

There's a prevailing sense that people are too wedded to the flow of information coming at them through their smart phones. Everyone talks about unplugging but very people are able to follow through. Our phones are intimate parts of our lives now, like it or not. So instead of asking people to unplug in order to focus on real life conversation and connection, why not find a way to use smartphones to enable and enhance real world connections? That's the core concept behind The Dinamundo Project. It's about connecting with people, not checking into places.

The iPhone app is straightforward - Players scan QR codes on each other's phones and are rewarded with a laugh as they read to each other from fortune cookies. They can share out the forutnes and meetngs on Facebook, and can keep track of the people they have met up with in the app. This is all pretty lightweight stuff. But underneath it are some pretty powerful principles.

Prototype

Proof of Concept

The Dinamundo Project started in a somewhat unconventional way. I first heard of the app in October 2011 from a friend who was peripherally involved. The first version had been released as a Facebook app and, while the concepts were there, the experience was a bit clunky. As my friend walked me through the app, we began to imagine what it might look like as a mobile experience. I went home that night and couldn't help myself from drawing up a few wireframes, inspired by our conversation that day. I sent them over to my friend and, to my surprise, he had coded the wireframes into a functional prototype the next day!

We continued going back and forth for a bit and by Thanksgiving we had nearly a fully functioning app! We figured it was time to show it to the original product owner. I wasn't sure how he was going to take it - after all, he didn't even know my name. But when my collegue showed him the app, he was pretty impressed. So much so that instead of winding down the Dinamundo Project, as he was thinking at the time, he decided to put more resources towards and he hired me and my collegue to run the startup team that would build it.

Research and Analysis

Digging In Deep

The prototype had convinced the product owner, Erik Blachford, to officially fund the development of the Dinamundo project rather than wrap the project up as he had previously been thinking. It had helped him see just how much potential there as to the concept and he had realized that, with the right team and resources behind it, it deserved a real shot.

Rather than jump right in to building the prototype though, he wanted to make sure our decisions were founded in solid research. And so my first task was to conduct and indepth market analysis of both the travel and checkin space in order to determine the right product positioning as well as gain a full understanding of the target user.

Industry Analysis

The first purpose of my research was to analyze check in functionalities in the context of the current travel landscape. I wanted to answer questions like - where and how are users checking in? How do current travel patterns influence where and how users will check in abroad?
