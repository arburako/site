Dinamundo

INTERACTION + VISUAL DESIGN

Overview

The Dinamundo iPhone app is based on a simple yet powerful principle - to use smartphones to enable and enhance our personal connections. Our phones are intimate parts of lives, like it or not. Instead of letting them constantly distract us from the world around us, we can use them to engage with our surroundings at a deeper level. That's the core concept behind Dinamundo, a travel check in game that uses popular gaming strategies to bring people together in the real world and help them create a meaningful connection.

My Role

The project took place from January 2012 through May 2012. Under the direction of Founder & CEO, startup veteran Erik Blachford, I led all facets of the product development lifecycle - strategy, interaction + visual design, execution process, etc - from concept ideation through product launch.

Challenge

The Original App

Dinamundo started back in 2011 with the mission of using modern technology to enhance and facilitate meaningful interactions in the real world, rather than distracting us from them.

Founder Erik Blachford (formed Expedia CEO), knew his way around the travel space and saw an opportunity to crack the traveler/local meet up nut, which still remains truly unbroken even today. Erik contracted developers from Pivotal Labs Agency to build the first iteration of his vision. As a one man show, Erik drove all details of the product development from communicating development and design requirements, to creating wireframes, and managing a cross-functional team through execution. He didn’t quit his day job.

In October 2011, Dinamundo Facebook Application where users could join and message other members. It was a simple platform only meant to be a starting point to a much loftier vision, but momentum was lacking and progress was slow. As the Pivotal contract drew to an end, Erik was beginning to wonder whether he could sustain his level of effort on Dinamundo development, or if it was even enough. Dinamundo was his passion project and he did not want to see it fail. But without much more wiggle room in his schedule, he didn’t see many other options.
