Dinamundo

INTERACTION + VISUAL DESIGN

About the Project

Dinamundo is an iPhone app that uses mobile technology to connect people through fun, light-weight moments. The iPhone app is straightforward – Players scan QR codes on each other’s phones and are rewarded with a laugh as they read to each other from fortune cookies. The can share out the fortunes and meetings on Facebook, and can keep track of the people they have meet up with from within the app.This is pretty lightweight stuff. But underneath it are some pretty powerful principles.

My Role

The project took place from January 2012 through May 2012 led by startup veteran, Erik Blachford. I was the Product Co-founder on the team of 4, responsible for all facets of the product development lifecycle from concept ideation through product launch.

Objective

Checking Into People

There’s a prevailing sense that people are too wedded to the flow of information coming at them through their smart phones. Everyone talks about unplugging but very few people are able to follow through. Our phones are intimate parts of our lives now, like it or not. So instead of asking people to unplug in order to focus on real life conversation and connection, why not find a way to use smartphones to enable and enhance real world connections? That’s the core concept behind The Dinamundo Project. It’s about connecting with people, not checking in to places.


mission: Use smartphones to encourage real world connections

strategy: Deliver fun and engagement when people connect


Research

Finding the right angle

Quantitative Data

As a first step in the process, I did loads of quantitative research. I tried to gather as much inofrmation as I could find on both the travel space - since the app was geared towards the traveler / local dynamic - and the check in space - since the app was meant to be a social / local game (ala Foursquare). I compiled my findings into a series of infographic visual representations to share with the team and inform future design decisions.
